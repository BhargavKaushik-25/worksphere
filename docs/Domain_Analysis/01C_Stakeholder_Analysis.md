# 1. Purpose

The Stakeholder Analysis document identifies all individuals, groups,
departments, and external entities that interact with or influence the
WorkSphere platform throughout its lifecycle.

The purpose of this document is to understand stakeholder responsibilities,
expectations, influence, communication requirements, and overall impact on
the successful delivery and operation of WorkSphere.

A clear understanding of stakeholders enables effective collaboration,
better decision-making, improved requirement gathering, and successful
project execution.

---

# 2. Scope

This document applies to all stakeholders associated with the WorkSphere
project during planning, development, testing, deployment, operation, and
maintenance.

The analysis includes both internal and external stakeholders who influence
business decisions, software development, infrastructure, security,
operations, compliance, and end-user adoption.

---

# 3. Objectives

The objectives of this stakeholder analysis are to:

- Identify all project stakeholders.
- Define stakeholder responsibilities.
- Understand stakeholder expectations.
- Evaluate stakeholder influence and interest.
- Improve communication between stakeholders.
- Support project planning and governance.
- Reduce stakeholder-related project risks.
- Facilitate successful adoption of the WorkSphere platform.

---

# 4. Stakeholder Identification

The WorkSphere platform involves multiple categories of stakeholders that
contribute to different phases of the project.

These stakeholders include business users, technical teams, project
management, external service providers, and organizational leadership.

Each stakeholder has unique responsibilities, expectations, authority, and
communication requirements that influence project success.

---

# 5. Stakeholder Categories

The stakeholders of WorkSphere are classified into the following categories:

## 5.1 Business Stakeholders

Business stakeholders define organizational goals, business requirements,
and operational expectations.

Examples include:

- Executive Management
- Department Managers
- Human Resources
- Employees
- Project Managers

---

## 5.2 Technical Stakeholders

Technical stakeholders are responsible for designing, developing,
deploying, maintaining, and securing the WorkSphere platform.

Examples include:

- Solution Architect
- Software Developers
- QA Engineers
- DevOps Engineers
- Database Administrators
- Security Administrators
- System Administrators

---

## 5.3 External Stakeholders

External stakeholders provide services or interact with WorkSphere from
outside the organization.

Examples include:

- Cloud Service Providers
- Third-Party Integration Providers
- External Auditors
- Technology Vendors

# 13. Stakeholder Engagement Strategy

The success of WorkSphere depends on continuous collaboration between
business stakeholders, technical teams, and external partners.

The following engagement principles shall be followed throughout the
project lifecycle:

- Engage executive stakeholders during major planning and approval phases.
- Conduct regular sprint reviews with the Product Owner.
- Involve department managers during requirement validation.
- Gather continuous feedback from employees through demonstrations and pilot releases.
- Hold architecture review meetings with technical stakeholders.
- Conduct security reviews before major releases.
- Share project progress transparently through dashboards and status reports.
- Encourage collaboration across cross-functional teams to ensure alignment with business goals.
